Mandarin system design · ADHD-friendly principles
⏱ Micro-tasks only Max 10–15 min per task block. Pomodoro 25-min sessions. No marathon study.
🔤 Pinyin first, always Every character shown with pinyin above. Tones colour-coded. Never bare characters alone.
🗂 Toggle everything Grammar detail, stroke order, extra examples — all collapsed by default. One idea per toggle.
🎙 Tone drilling weekly At least 1 recorded tone + speaking milestone per week. The 4 tones are non-negotiable from day 1.
☀ Light Sundays Review-only, listening immersion, or rest. Never a full study day — same rule as Japanese.
📱 Tools in use Quizlet (SRS vocab) · HelloChinese or Duolingo (habit) · Notion (day pages) · No Anki.
Day page structure · Mandarin (canonical)
1 · Metadata HSK Level · Vocab count · Time · Skills · XP available
2 · Session timeline Block · Time · Activity (identical format to JP)
3 · Tone warm-up 0–5 min · 3 micro-tasks · 4 tones + neutral
4 · Morning blocks Grammar · Vocab drills · Character writing · Toggles
5 · Evening block Quizlet SRS · Listening · Speaking drill · Tone review
6–8 · Close-out Checklist · Before-next-day tasks · Closing line in Mandarin
Closing lines use pinyin + characters. Example: 你今天做得很好！(Nǐ jīntiān zuò de hěn hǎo!) — same warmth as the Japanese 〜お疲れ様でした pattern.
